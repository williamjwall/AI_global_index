About the AI Global Index Project

The AI Global Index Project is a comprehensive initiative evaluating the landscape of artificial intelligence. Analyzing AI publications from 2010 to 2022 reveals a substantial surge, driven by breakthroughs and collaborative research. GitHub stars reflect the popularity of AI libraries, with TensorFlow leading. The Index categorizes aspects like Talent, Infrastructure, and Commercial viability, assigning scores. Notably, the high Research score highlights a vibrant research ecosystem, driven by breakthroughs like GPT-2 and BERT. GitHub stars and AI scores provide valuable insights, guiding strategic decisions and offering a nuanced understanding of the global AI ecosystem.

    The surge in AI-related publications over the past decade underscores the remarkable growth and impact of artificial intelligence on the global research landscape. In 2010, there were 162,444 publications, and each subsequent year witnessed a steady increase, reaching a projected count of 350,000 in 2022. This exponential rise in publication numbers reflects the expanding interest and engagement of the research community in advancing AI technologies. Major breakthroughs, such as the dominance of deep learning in 2012 and AlphaGo's victory in 2015, have not only fueled the curiosity of researchers but have also contributed to the prolific dissemination of knowledge in the field. The collaborative nature of AI development, coupled with breakthroughs like GPT-2, BERT, and AlphaFold, has led to a rich tapestry of research contributions, driving the evolution of artificial intelligence and shaping its multifaceted applications across various industries.
    GitHub stars serve as a measure of a repository's popularity and community support within the developer and AI communities. Topping the chart is 'TensorFlow' with an impressive 160,672 stars, showcasing its widespread adoption and influence in the AI domain. Following closely are 'Pytorch' and 'Keras' with 52,745 and 53,218 stars respectively, highlighting the significant traction these libraries have gained. Other notable entries include 'OpenCV,' 'Scikit-learn,' 'DeepLearning-500-questions,' and 'TensorFlow-Examples,' each contributing to the rich ecosystem of tools and resources available for AI development. The cumulative GitHub stars offer valuable insights into the preferences and trust developers place in these libraries for building and exploring artificial intelligence applications.
